Project Overview

Objective

Apply forensic data analytics to identify temporal patterns in serial homicide data, demonstrating how modern techniques could enable earlier detection of criminal behavior within healthcare systems.

Data

215 confirmed murder cases from the Shipman Inquiry (1975-1998). Temporal data reconstructed from death certificates, witness statements, and medical records.

Tools & Technologies

Python pandas matplotlib seaborn Jupyter Chart.js

Academic Context

Master's in Data Analytics capstone project demonstrating advanced pattern recognition, statistical analysis, and ethical data handling.

Key Findings

55.8%

Afternoon Murders

Over half of all murders occurred between 12 PM and 6 PM, with a distinct peak at 4:00 PM (38 victims).

88.8%

Weekday Bias

Nearly 90% of murders occurred Monday through Friday, with Monday being the peak day (51 victims).

60.9%

Seasonal Concentration

Over 60% of murders occurred in Spring and Summer months, suggesting strategic timing.

4:00 PM

Peak Hour

Highest concentration of murders occurred at 4:00 PM, with 38 victims (7.6× average hour).

Temporal Pattern Analysis

Time of Day Distribution

Clear afternoon concentration with distinct peaks at 10 AM, 11 AM, 2 PM, and 4 PM. Note the complete absence of nighttime murders.

Day of Week Pattern Average

Strong workweek pattern with Monday peak (51 victims) and dramatic weekend drop (only 24 victims total).

Seasonal Distribution

Spring and Summer account for 61% of all murders, matching patterns of increased patient isolation during warmer months.

Time Pattern Heatmap

Interaction between day of week and time of day shows consistent patterns across all weekdays.

Methodology

1

Data Acquisition & Cleaning

Collected 215 confirmed cases from official inquiry records. Standardized temporal formats, validated consistency, and created categorical variables for analysis.

# Data cleaning snippet df['Hour'] = pd.to_datetime(df['TimeOfDeath']).dt.hour df['DayOfWeek'] = pd.to_datetime(df['DateOfDeath']).dt.day_name() df['Season'] = df['Month'].apply(get_season)
2

Statistical Analysis

Applied descriptive statistics, frequency analysis, and hypothesis testing to identify significant patterns and anomalies.

# Statistical testing chi2, p_value = stats.chisquare(observed_frequencies) z_scores = (hourly_counts - hourly_counts.mean()) / hourly_counts.std() anomalies = z_scores[abs(z_scores) > 3]
3

Pattern Recognition

Used temporal clustering algorithms to identify distinct patterns in murder timing and developed novel metrics for pattern strength.
